Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

The most commonly used sign to replace a motorcycle helmet is to check the age of the helmet. If your helmet is older than five years, there is a very good chance that you might have been missing out on a lot of new features and innovations that have been made in the helmet industry.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Take your bike to your local bike shop for a quick safety check. They can determine if it is safe to ride or needs any additional maintenance. Ask them for an estimate first before the work is done. They can call you if they discover something major in the inspection process.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow the Law. Be Predictable. Be Conspicuous. Think Ahead. Ride Ready
